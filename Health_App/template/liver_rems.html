Factors that put people at increased risk for heart disease are

Smoking, High Blood Pressure, High Cholesterol, Diabetes, Family history of heart problems especially heart attacks and strokes, Obesity


HEART DISEASE SYMPTOMS:

Chest Pain (Angina)

It is described a crushing pain or heaviness in the center of the chest with radiation of the pain to the arm (usually the left) or jaw.

difficulty breathing, fatigue and light headedness

Women, people who have diabetes, and the elderly may have different pain perceptions and may complain of overwhelming fatigue and weakness or a change in their ability to perform routine daily activities like walking, climbing steps, or doing household chores.

swelling due to fluid retention, or edema

HOW TO PREVENT HEART DISEASE?

Eating a balanced diet

Opt for a heart-healthy diet that is rich in fiber and favors whole grains and fresh fruits and vegetables. Also, limit the intake of processed foods and added fat, salt, and sugar.

Maintaining a moderate body weight

Being overweight especially around your middle increases your risk of heart disease. Excess weight can lead to conditions that increase your chances of developing heart disease, including high blood pressure, high cholesterol and type 2 diabetes.

Exercising regularly

This can help strengthen the heart and circulatory system, reduce cholesterol, and maintain blood pressure.

Get good quality sleep

A lack of sleep can do more than leave you yawning; it can harm your health. People who don't get enough sleep have a higher risk of obesity, high blood pressure, heart attack, diabetes and depression

Get regular health screenings

Seek treatment for conditions that affect heart health, such as high blood pressure, obesity, and diabetes.

Quitting smoking

Smoking is a major risk factor for heart and cardiovascular conditions.
