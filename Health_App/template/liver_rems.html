Factors that may increase your risk of liver disease include

Heavy alcohol use, obesity, Type 2 diabetes, injecting drugs using shared needles, exposure to certain chemicals or toxins, family history of liver disease.


LIVER DISEASE SYMPTOMS:

Skin and eyes that appear yellowish (jaundice)

Abdominal pain and swelling

Swelling in the legs and ankles

Dark urine color, pale stool color

Nausea or vomiting, chronic fatigue,

Loss of appetite



HOW TO PREVENT LIVER DISEASE?

Protect your skin

When using insecticides and other toxic chemicals, wear gloves, long sleeves, a hat and a mask so that chemicals aren't absorbed through your skin.

Maintain a healthy weight

Being overweight especially around your middle increases your risk of liver disease. Obesity can cause nonalcoholic fatty liver disease.

Eating a balanced diet

Opt for a healthy diet that is rich in fiber and favors whole grains and fresh fruits and vegetables. Also, limit the intake of processed foods and added fat, salt, and sugar.

Keep your food safe

Wash your hands thoroughly before eating or preparing foods.

Get vaccinated

If you're at increased risk of contracting hepatitis or if you've already been infected with any form of the hepatitis virus, talk to your doctor about getting the hepatitis A and hepatitis B vaccines.

Use medications wisely

ake prescription and nonprescription drugs only when needed and only in recommended doses. Don't mix medications and alcohol.
